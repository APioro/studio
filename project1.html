Challange



User Concerns

  • Navigation feels cluttered and overwhelming, leaving users unsure of where to begin or how to proceed.
  • The website is frustrating to browse, becasue of slow loading.
  • Lot of disorganised content (blog posts, lab research result, product information, success stories) makes it difficult to find relevant information.

Company Goals

  • Improve Conversion
  • Build trust by showcasing the benefits of Absolute Collagen supplements through educational content and lab results.
  • Achieve a recognisable and unified branding on their digital spaces

How Do We Know?

User Interviews

I observed users’ first interactions with the website and how they struggle to complete given tasks. Many users expressed feeling overwhelmed and indicated they wanted to leave before finishing.


Google Analytics

High bounce rates confirmed the issues observed. Additionally, low interaction with educational content and long time spent in the navigation indicate problems with site structure.


Stakeholders

Customer service team frequently received inquiries about the product’s qualities, even though the answers were available on the website.






Constraints



Limited Resources & Data

I lacked access to user session recordings or analytics specific to the target audience.
Additionally, I didn’t receive branding assets, photography, or a formal brand style guide.

Workaround:

  • Conducted in-person observations at my university to gather usability feedback from a diverse audience.
  • Analyzed competitor websites to identify industry standards and improve design strategy.
  • Reverse-engineered a style guide by studying existing website visuals to ensure consistency.

Limited Stakeholder Feedback

The stakeholder was unable to provide timely feedback during the process, which caused delays in decision-making and progress.

Workaround:

  • I focused on conducting usability tests independently and gathering as much actionable data as possible to support my design decisions.

Research



Questions That Guided My Research


1. What are the main actions users perform through the website?

2. How do users categorize and seek information?

3. What information is essential for users before making a purchase of a suplement?


Card Sorting



Sample of 16 participants.
Purpose: To identify how users naturally group website content and determine intuitive categories for improved navigation and information structure.




Mistakes...



Not Explaining Category Purpose

I didn’t clarify that the categories should be organized with navigation in mind. In the future, I will provide more context in the instructions.

Unclear Phrasing of Cards

I aimed to make the statements easy to digest, but in an attempt to anonymize the brand, I replaced it with "X." Unfortunately, this choice made the statements harder to follow and detracted from the clarity of the task. Many participants abandoned the research. This experience taught me the importance of balancing privacy concerns with clarity.




Conclusions:

1. What are the main actions users perform through the website?

2. How do users categorize and seek information?

3. What information is essential for users before making a purchase of a suplement?
